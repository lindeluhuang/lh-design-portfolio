The Patient Journey

Problem

Healthcare is an extremely confusing landscape, and patients who try to navigate the system are doing so under increased stress and anxiety due to their conditions. But considering healthcare is inherently personal and sensitive in nature, it’s critical to make sure the patients are as supported as possible throughout the process.

The University of Michigan Hospital has created many of its clinical workflows to maximize efficiency of the hospital’s operations, creating unintended consequences for the experience of the patient. This has created a severe lack of awareness from each department about what exactly takes place in other departments, clinics, or services a patient is exposed to.

Proposal

We need a holistic understanding of the patient’s journey through the health system in order to effectively engage patients as full partners in their care.

We hope to create a patient journey map for a number of different patient experiences throughout the University of Michigan hospital system. Having this visual framework will help us better understand areas within a journey that cause distress and unnecessary confusion so we can work to improve them, as well as identify opportunities for impactful engagement.

High-level Plan

  1. Conduct contextual research with Patient and Family Advisors, which could include a host of methods (interviews, diary studies, ethnographic observations, etc.)
  2. Synthesize research findings
  3. Graphically visualize a journey map
  4. Disseminate the process and findings (paper, presentations, etc.)
  5. Begin design projects that address key problems identified through the study

Partners

We're working on this project in partnership with the University of Michigan’s Office of Patient Experience.
