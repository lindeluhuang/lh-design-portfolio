Wearables for Hypertension

Problem

The use of wearables to track fitness data is on the rise, but not all populations have equal access to the technology and its benefits. While many research studies have been conducted on wealthier populations with easy access to these devices, little work has been done to test the effectiveness of mobile health in underprivileged populations.

One common health issue in these underprivileged areas is a high prevalence of hypertension. This condition increases the risk for heart disease and stroke, proving to be a major public health crisis in these communities.

Proposal

We aim to study the effectiveness of various types of mobile health interventions for the management of hypertension, focusing on increasing exercise and minimizing salt in the diet.

While we aim to study the effectiveness of these mobile health solutions, we are focusing on finding systems that work for the Flint community, a population that has been largely underserved by major healthcare centers.

High-level Plan

  1. Strategize: Establish audience and goals.
  2. Discover: Gain a deep understanding of core users, consolidating the initial learnings to identify and prioritize user needs.
  3. Prototype: Create multiple possibilities of mobile health technologies and service models, test them with users.
  4. Build: Create a minimum viable product to release to the world, tracking key metrics and garnering feedback to guide future improvements.

Partners

We're working on this project in partnership with the University of Michigan’s Wearables In Reducing Risk and Enhancing Daily Lifestyle Center (WIRED-L Center).
