The COVID-19 Recovery Experience

Problem

The COVID-19 pandemic has ravaged the world. While there have been a number of scientific studies on the clinical effects of the virus in the body, few research studies have tried to gain a holistic understanding of the entire COVID experience.

Proposal

There is more to surviving a dangerous pandemic than overcoming the physical condition, and we want to gain a deep understanding of the mental, physical, and social aspects of what it is like to recover from COVID.

We aim to conduct a mixed-methods design research study that examines COVID survivors from a variety of locations in the US and Canada including Los Angeles, San Francisco, Chicago, Michigan, and Toronto.

By examining the recovery experiences within and between regions, we hope to uncover insights that may lead to better COVID recovery now and in the future.

High-level Plan

  1. Conduct a series of interviews with COVID survivors, including people who were hospitalized and people who recovered at home
  2. Synthesize research findings of people in Michigan
  3. Synthesize research findings of people across the US and Canada
  4. Disseminate the process and findings (paper, presentations, etc.)
  5. Begin design projects that address key problems identified through the study

Partners

We're working on this project in partnership with design researchers at The Better Lab at UCSF, UCLA Health, Rush University Medical Center, and North York General Hospital.
