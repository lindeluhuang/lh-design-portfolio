About Me

Hello there! I'm Linde. (It rhymes with "windy.") My road to UX/UI has been a long and winding one. Let me take you on my journey...

As a student at Duke University, I studied Electrical and Computer Engineering with the hopes of becoming a biomedical researcher. (Both my parents have PhDs in engineering, so that's just what you do right?) I spent my summers gaining research experience, even publishing a first-author paper under Dr. Steven Cummer exploring metamaterials, amazing structures that can see through walls and make things invisible.

Upon graduation, I got the chance to interview at Microsoft and a landed job as a Program Manager. It wasn't research, but working on products that hundreds of millions of people use was an opportunity too great to pass up. I spent two years creating new features for Windows and Office, including consolidating the Windows audio pipeline and redesigning the Office support website.

After a couple years in the corporate world, I felt it was time to go back to my research roots. I found an opportunity to work with Dr. Andreas Velten, using the technology behind his camera that can see around corners to aid medical diagnostics, like early detection of breast cancer.

However, after a few months I realized I wanted to solve real, tangible problems rather than work on abstract research. I then transitioned to Epic, a company that makes the country's most comprehensive Electronic Health Record. Working on the data analytics team, I gained a deep understanding of how to store and utilize data in databases. Through in-person training, eLearning curricula, and in-product tutorials, I taught the inner workings of the Epic database to health organization staff.

Throughout my work experiences, I've always been drawn to design. At Microsoft I worked on a user-facing product, working collaboratively with my UX and UI designers on new website features. At Epic I became the team's informal graphic designer, being called on to create complex diagrams and templates for class presentations. And in my personal life, I create all manner of designs for my friends and family.

This summer, I got married and moved to Ann Arbor, Michigan, giving me the chance to transition to Design as a full-time gig. I'm working through the Block Designer Track to learn UX, UI, and front-end development, adding structure and rigor to the design repertoire I've developed through the years.

In my next role, I hope to use all breadth of my full-stack design skills as a UX/UI designer. I want to make products people find easy to use and, dare I say, even enjoy using. If my long and winding road piques your interest, I look forward to connecting with you soon.
