Community Engagement Network

Problem

Health research has long been considered solely an academic pursuit, and it’s extremely common for researchers to conduct their experiments without the contributions and feedback of the greater communities they study. This makes for both bad relationships and bad science.

Thankfully, a new research approach is starting to gain traction. Community-engaged research (CEnR) is a philosophy and set of research methods that aim to bring community partners into the research process, at any point from study design and implementation to analysis and dissemination.

However, since the approach is so new, there is very little infrastructure in place to help it take root and thrive.

Proposal

We aim to use a human-centered design approach to create a network that will enable and enhance community-engaged research across the state of Michigan.

This product we build will likely be a dynamic system consisting of both a technological component as well as a human service model that outlines how the technology can seamlessly integrate with the existing MICHR Community Engagement team offerings.

High-level Plan

  1. Strategize: Establish audience and goals
  2. Discover: Gain a deep understanding of core users, consolidating the initial learnings to identify and prioritize user needs
  3. Prototype: Create multiple possibilities of platforms and service models, test them with users
  4. Build: Create a minimum viable product to release to the world, tracking key metrics and garnering feedback to guide future improvements.

Partners

We're working on this project in partnership with the Michigan Institute of Clinical & Health Research (MICHR) Community Engagement team.
